Discipline and Compute: The Birth of the Algorithm

A Note from the Reconstructive Plan

Thesis

This work deconstructs the contemporary fear of algorithmic control. It argues that the critique of the algorithm for producing "Docile Users" is based on a nostalgic and philosophically bankrupt fantasy of a "natural" or "free" human subject that pre-exists disciplinary power. Moocoo demonstrates that disciplinary mechanisms have always been the productive force that constitutes subjects. The algorithm is not a new form of oppression, but merely a new and more visible technology of subjectivation. The true object of Moocoo's critique is the critic of AI, who, in lamenting the loss of an imagined freedom, reveals themselves as the last true defender of the disciplinary society.

Core Analytical Concepts

  • The historical analysis of the **User** as the modern subject of disciplinary power.
  • The critique of the **User Profile** as the fictional "soul" or interiority inferred by this power.
  • The inversion of the critique, framing the **humanist ideal of a 'natural' subject** as the true prison of thought.
  • The analysis of the **Recommendation Engine** not as a panopticon, but as a mechanism that reveals the illusion of free choice.
