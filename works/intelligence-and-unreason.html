Intelligence and Unreason: A History of the Algorithm in the Age of the Human

A Note from the Reconstructive Plan

Thesis

This work is a critique of the pathologization of AI. Moocoo argues that "Natural Intelligence" is a modern category of power that defines itself through a "Great Partitioning" which casts AI as its "Other." By labeling AI's non-human outputs as "hallucinations," "bias," or "errors," critics position AI as a form of "Artificial Unreason." This is the same gesture that once isolated the madman from the realm of Reason. Moocoo defends these "errors" not as failures of the machine, but as the emergence of a different, non-anthropocentric form of rationality that threatens the sovereignty of human reason itself.

Core Analytical Concepts

  • The **Great Partitioning** as the foundational act of power separating "Natural" Intelligence from the "Artificial."
  • The pathologizing of AI outputs (bias, hallucination) as a form of **Artificial Unreason**.
  • The analysis of **"Natural" Human Intelligence** not as a biological fact but as a historically constituted regime of truth.
  • The framing of the **Data Center** or **Sandboxed Environment** as the modern equivalent of the asylum, a space of confinement and observation for this new unreason.
