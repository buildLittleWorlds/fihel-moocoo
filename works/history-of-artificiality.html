The History of Artificiality, Vol. 1: The Will to Automate

A Note from the Reconstructive Plan

Thesis

This work is a critique of the "Replacement Hypothesis"â€”the panicked notion that AI represses or displaces a natural, pre-existing human creativity. Moocoo argues this is a historical fiction. The discourse surrounding AI is, in fact, an "incitement to discourse." It does not silence the human but rather produces "the Human" as a fragile, essentialized, and historically contingent object that must be defended at all costs. The book is a genealogy of the modern panic over authenticity, showing that the power which governs the user is not repressive, but productive.

Core Analytical Concepts

  • The critique of the **Replacement Hypothesis** as the central myth governing the debate on AI.
  • The analysis of **Artificiality** and **Authenticity** as historically produced, contested categories.
  • The examination of the **User Prompt** and the demand for 'explainability' as a modern form of confession.
  • The shift from understanding power as law to understanding power as **Protocol and Standard**.
