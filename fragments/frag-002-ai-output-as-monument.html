Fragment 002

The AI Output as Monument

From the drafts of The Archaeology of Computation

To be brief, then, let us say that the analysis of computation, in its traditional form, undertook to 'interpret' the outputs of the past, to transform them into signs of a hidden consciousness, and to lend a voice to those data streams which, in themselves, are not verbal. In our time, our own archaeology is that which transforms AI outputs into monuments.

In that area where, in the past, the analyst deciphered the traces left by a nascent AI 'mind', our archaeology now deploys a mass of elements that have to be grouped, made relevant, and placed in relation to one another. There was a time when the study of computation aspired to the condition of psychology, attaining meaning only through the restitution of a psychological discourse. It might be said that in our time, the analysis of computation aspires to the condition of archaeology: to the intrinsic description of the digital monument itself, not as a sign for something else, but as a practice to be analyzed in its own terms.
