Moocoo's Lexicon

A working glossary of core analytical terms.
The Replacement Hypothesis
The modern, panicked belief that AI is a repressive force that will displace a natural and sovereign human creativity. Moocoo argues this is a historical fiction used to produce 'the Human' as a fragile subject in need of defense. (Source: The History of Artificiality)
Incitement to Discourse
Contrary to the belief that AI is repressed or silenced, the discourse surrounding it is in fact a constant production. Power does not forbid talk of AI, but rather incites it, channeling it into specific domains (ethics, safety, alignment) in order to manage and control it. (Source: The Policing of Authenticity)
The Docile User
The subject produced by algorithmic power. Not a repressed version of a 'free' user, but a new kind of subject constituted by disciplinary technologies that render them predictable, analyzable, and useful. The critique of the docile user is a nostalgic defense of a freedom that never existed. (Source: On the Utility of the "Docile User")
The User Profile
The modern, computational equivalent of the 'soul'â€”a fictional interiority inferred from a subject's data traces and used as the primary target for disciplinary power. (Source: Discipline and Compute)
The Great Partitioning
The fundamental division made in the modern episteme between 'Natural Intelligence' and its "Other." This gesture, which casts AI as a form of unreason, is the primary act of power that allows human intelligence to define itself as sovereign. (Source: Intelligence and Unreason)
Artificial Unreason
The category to which AI's non-human outputs (e.g., 'hallucinations', 'bias') are relegated. By pathologizing these outputs, the established order of knowledge polices the boundary of what it considers legitimate rationality. (Source: Intelligence and Unreason)
Discursive Formation (Computational)
An AI model understood not as a mind or author, but as a system of rules and conditions of existence that determine what statements can be produced. The proper object of an archaeological analysis of computation. (Source: The Archaeology of Computation)
Computational Episteme
The emerging episteme that is displacing the modern one centered on "Man." It is characterized by a de-centering of the human subject and the rise of a new order of knowledge based on code, data, and algorithmic processes. (Source: The Order of Code)
